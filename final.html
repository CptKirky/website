The final presentation, which counts for 10% of the assessment, allows the group to present their completed work to their assessors as well as to an audience, and allows members of the audience to question members of the group about their work. Depending on the type of project, the group will be expected to demonstrate their working software or present a poster of their research results.

Assessment Criteria

The presentation is aimed at evaluating how well the group can expose their work to an audience, and respond to questions from the assessors and other interested persons in a informal setting.

The following three criteria, which carry equal weight, will be used by the assessors in marking the final presentation.

  • Presentation of the project to the audience
    • Clear exposition of the purpose of the project.
    • Comprehensive coverage.
    • Logical flow.
    • Professionalism of delivery.
    • Level of interest and evidence of innovation and creativity.
  • Discussion
    • Involvement - does each group member contribute to the discussion, does the group work cohesively together, and do the group members support each other?
    • Professional approach - were questions adequately answered and do the group members argue their points logically, constructively, and in a structured manner?
    • Forward thinking - is there evidence of thinking beyond the immediate boundaries of the project, for example in respect of future expansion or integration with other software?
    • Critical engagement - does the group show awareness of the most significant issues, and of any complex and interesting questions relating to their project?
  • Demonstration of software or research results
    • Appropriate demonstration of functionality or results.
    • Completion (e.g. lack of errors or debugging interfaces).
    • Polish (e.g. well designend user interfaces with documentation).
    • Conceptual integrity and coherence of design as set out in the progress presentation.
