The progress presentation, which counts for 20% of the assessment, allows the group to present their progress and have a constructive discussion with their assessors.

The presentation itself should convey the following information to the assessors:

  • technical description of the project;
  • current specification for product, as emerging from interaction with customer;
  • details of progress to date;
  • indication of prospects and plans for future work;
  • description of the personnel and their roles in the management of the project.

If a group wishes to present other material (such as to demonstrate prototype software on a laptop) they may do so, but it will not affect the marks received.

Groups should be aware that assessors viewing the presentation may not be familiar with the topic, and the overall "plan" for the project should be clear from the presentation.

By the end of the first week of Term 2 (at the latest) a feedback sheet on the progress presentation together with a final mark for this assessment stage of the project will be returned to each group via Tabula.

Practical information

The progress presentation will last (up to) 1 hour. Around 30 and 40 minutes should be spent presenting. The remaining time is for interaction and questions with the assessors (and customer/s, if present). Time will be allowed for setting up before the demonstration. The project moderator acts as chair for the presentation.

Assessment Criteria

The progress presentation allows us to monitor the project to ensure that the group is making suitable progress, and to offer you feedback which will help bring the project to a successful conclusion. The presentation should give an overview of the project, its motivation, its background, the design decisions made, and its current status. It is not intended to be a draft of part of the final presentation or the final report.

The following four criteria, which carry equal weight, will be used by the assessors in marking the progress presentation.

  • Technical content
    • Students well read and knowledgeable in the project's subject area.
    • Good insight into the project's specific topic.
    • Effective analysis of problems and issues given the customer requirements.
    • Quality of design work.
    • Appropriate choice of methods and tools.
  • Progress
    • Progress consistent with the project specification.
    • Initially unforeseen problems well detected and overcome.
  • Project Organisation and Management
    • All necessary research, analysis, and design work on schedule.
    • Team management.
    • Communication with, and responsiveness to the customer.
    • Work for next term well planned out.
  • Communication Skills
    • Basic written language skills such as spelling and grammar.
    • Effective composition in the context of a poster.
    • Effective dialogue with the assessors.
